\begin{cvletter}

I am writing to express my strong interest in the "Associate Gameplay Engineer" program at Maxis. With a background as an indie game developer and a researcher specialized in simulation, I am eager to contribute my expertise to the world of "The Sims."

My journey began in the realm of physics, where I honed my proficiency in technical areas including C, Python, Linux systems, supercomputing, and simulations. These skills have adeptly equipped me to tackle complex technical challenges. Building upon this foundation, my PhD research took a unique trajectory by combining my expertise in computational social science, leading me to develop equation-based and agent-based models for studying society.

During my PhD journey, my fervor for simulation games drove me to invest substantial effort in creating a sophisticated simulation framework and a game using Kotlin. This turn-based strategy game unfolds within a relativistic spacetime, introducing innovative gameplay designs. For instance, I implemented local economies instead of a global market, preventing faster-than-light information travel. Accomplishing a diverse array of gameplay mechanics such as economy, military strategy, politics, diplomacy, and utility-based AI while adhering to the game's unique requirements is an accomplishment I hold with pride. This effectively demonstrates my ability to excel in developing intricate data-driven games.

Additionally, I crafted a user-friendly GUI for both PC and Android platforms. I prioritized algorithm optimization and rigorous unit testing to uphold solid engineering practices. My active engagement in open-source projects, particularly within the realm of open-source gaming as reflected in my GitHub profile, underscores my commitment to evolving from a scientific programmer to a well-rounded software engineer.

The prospect of contributing my skills to "The Sims" fills me with anticipation. I am genuinely eager to embrace the opportunity to bring my expertise to your esteemed team. Thank you for considering my application.

\end{cvletter}
