I am writing to express my enthusiastic interest in the "Competitive Intelligence Analyst/Technical Analyst (MSc, PhD)" position at ASML. The opportunity to explore a role beyond my usual job-search scope has piqued my curiosity, and I am thrilled to discover how well my background aligns with the job requirements.

My educational foundation lies in physics. While my research experience may not be in semiconductor physics, my strong physics background enables me to quickly grasp intricate concepts and comprehend relevant literature. While I may not conduct semiconductor research myself, I am confident in my ability to derive insightful questions from the information I consume.

My doctoral studies encompass quantitative science studies. Although my primary research focus diverges from scientific/patent literature analysis, I possess a solid grasp of essential quantitative techniques such as citation analysis and textual analysis. Leveraging these techniques could undoubtedly contribute to the fulfillment of the job responsibilities.

My academic journey has consistently exposed me to individuals from diverse backgrounds and nationalities. This interaction, coupled with my recent involvement in organizing conferences, workshops, and local community events, has substantially honed my communication and interpersonal skills.

Having grown up in Hong Kong, I am a native in Chinese, proficient in both traditional and simplified Chinese. My fluency in Cantonese and Mandarin adds depth to my linguistic capabilities. Moreover, my Hong Kong citizenship could facilitate any necessary security clearances.

As an avid internet user, I have been close following the semiconductor industry's developments, particularly the ongoing dynamics within the Western vs Chinese semiconductor landscape. Joining ASML, a key player in this domain, would enable me to contribute meaningfully to this significant industry narrative.

Thank you for considering my application. I am excited about the prospect of contributing my unique skill set to ASML and I am eagerly looking forward to the opportunity to discuss my potential contributions further.
